Rosalita Cantina — Business Review & Turnaround Plan

Prepared: June 28, 2026 For: Owner / Management Review Data Period: January–May 2026 (actuals) with projections through June 2027 Data Source: Rosallita Cashflow May 24th 2026.xlsx, menu.txt, rosalitasbali.com, competitive research, AI industry data

Executive Summary

Rosalita Cantina is at a critical inflection point. The first five months of 2026 resulted in a cumulative EBITDA loss of approximately ‑IDR 443 million. However, the trajectory has been sharply improving:

  • January: ‑IDR 129.9M ← crisis
  • April: ‑IDR 43.9M ← improving
  • May: +IDR 434K ← first profitable month

The business model works. The positive EBITDA in May proves it. The problem was that the business was severely overstaffed and over-complicated for its current revenue volume. Corrective action has already begun (staff reduced from 34 → 22 between January and May) and must continue methodically.

Step 2.5 — Launch Daily Happy Hour (4–7PM)

IDR 15-30M/mo

This is the single biggest untapped time slot. Currently Rosalita's generates little revenue between lunch and dinner. The Thursday 5-8PM promo (shots + DJ) already proves there's demand for early-evening drinking.

Happy Hour Offer (daily, 4–7PM):

  • 20% off all signature cocktails and margaritas (Rosaritas, classic margs)
  • Half-price selected appetizers (Chips & Dip, Nachos Queso, Chicken Fingers)
  • Beer special: Bintang or Singaraja for IDR 35K (normally 60K/45K)

Why this works:

  • Drinks have 75-85% margin — even at 20% off, they still have 55-65% margin
  • Half-price appetizers drive food revenue at marginal cost
  • Creates a "pre-dinner" occasion — guests often stay for dinner after happy hour
  • Competes directly with Potato Head's and KU DE TA's late afternoon crowd

Target metrics:

  • 15–25 happy hour covers/day (currently ~0)
  • Average spend IDR 120–180K per HH guest
  • 20% conversion of HH guests to staying for dinner

Expected revenue: IDR 15–30M/month new revenue at 60%+ margin
