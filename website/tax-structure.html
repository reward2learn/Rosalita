The 5-Year Clock

Indonesian tax law allows losses to be carried forward for 5 years maximum. Carryback is not permitted. This means:

  • 2026 losses must be fully utilized by 2031 or they expire worthless
  • The turnaround plan targets profitability in mid-2027 — this aligns well with the 5-year window
  • If the realistic EBITDA target is hit (IDR 150-200M/month by mid-2027), the full loss pool can be utilized within 12-18 months of reaching profitability

General Indonesian Tax Context

Standard corporate tax rules every F&B business in Bali should know:

Rule Detail
Corporate Income Tax 22% standard rate (2026). Payable on annual net profit.
Loss carryforward 5 years maximum. No carryback. No group offsetting.
VAT (PPN) 11% on most goods and services. Input VAT on purchases is creditable against output VAT collected.
Withholding Tax (Article 23) 2% on management/consulting/technical service fees paid to Indonesian residents; 15% on interest and royalties.
Withholding Tax (Article 21) Progressive rates (5-35%) on employee salaries, withheld and remitted monthly.
Monthly filings PPN returns due end of following month. PPh Article 21/23 due by month's 20th/10th.
Annual CIT return Due April 30 for calendar-year filers. Monthly installments based on prior year's return.

Management Fee (IDR 6.25M/Month) — General Documentation Note

Rosalita pays IDR 6.25M/month in Admin/Management Fees (IDR 75M/year). This appears as a fixed expense in the cashflow workbook. General best practice for any significant business expense:

  • Keep the underlying agreement or invoice from the service provider
  • Ensure the fee corresponds to actual services received (management support, accounting, branding, etc.)
  • A flat unchanging fee during loss-making periods is common for third-party management contracts but should be documented
  • If paid to an Indonesian resident company: 2% withholding tax (Article 23) applies, and the recipient should issue an 11% PPN invoice

This is not a tax risk if properly documented — just ensure the paperwork is in order.
