## Executive Summary

Rosalita Cantina is at a critical inflection point. The first five months of 2026 resulted in a cumulative EBITDA loss of approximately **‑IDR 443 million**. However, the trajectory has been sharply improving:

- January: **‑IDR 129.9M** ← crisis
- April: **‑IDR 43.9M** ← improving
- May: **+IDR 434K** ← **first profitable month**

**The business model works.** The positive EBITDA in May proves it. The problem was that the business was severely overstaffed and over-complicated for its current revenue volume. Corrective action has already begun (staff reduced from 34 → 22 between January and May) and must continue methodically.

### The Five Levers to Sustainable Profitability

| # | Lever | EBITDA Impact | Timeline |
|---|-------|--------------|----------|
| **1** | **Fix loss-leading promotions** — Monday 25% off and Wednesday 67% off wings are destroying margin. Restructure to bundles. | **Protects IDR 10–18M/month** (immediate, zero cost) | **This week** |
| **2** | **Deploy free AI tools** — ChatGPT + Canva AI for marketing, social media, review responses, SOPs. Saves 15–20 hrs/week. | **Adds IDR 5–10M/month** (free to start) | **This week** |
| **3** | **Complete staff restructuring** — 22 → 16 FTE + 7shifts AI scheduling to optimize further | **Saves IDR 50–70M/month** | **3 months** |
| **4** | **Launch new revenue streams** — daily Happy Hour (4-7PM), weekend brunch, late-night weekend hours, hotel concierge partnerships | **Adds IDR 60–130M/month new revenue** | **2–6 months** |
| **5** | **Increase average spend** — upsell training, combo bundles, cocktail menu optimization, price adjustments on under-priced items | **Adds IDR 80–120M/month revenue lift** | **6–12 months** |

With all five levers in place by June 2027, the business should generate **IDR 150–200M+/month EBITDA** (conservative floor: IDR 101M; aspirational: IDR 258M) on **IDR 700–820M/month revenue** — a sustainable 20–30%+ EBITDA margin.

## Part B: Step-by-Step Action Plan

### Phase 1: Stop the Bleeding (July – September 2026)

These actions address the immediate cost structure and require no investment.

#### Step 1.1 — Complete Staff Restructuring

**Current (May):** 22 staff across all roles
**Target by September 2026:** 16–17 core FTE + casuals for peaks

| Role | Current | Target | Monthly Saving |
|------|---------|--------|---------------|
| Management | 1 (from Jul) | 1 | — |
| Shift Supervisor | 0 | 1 | (reallocate) |
| Reception/Cashier | 2 | 2 | — |
| Waiters/Waitresses | 5 | 4 | IDR 4M |
| Bar Staff | 3 (from Jul) | 2 | IDR 4.5M |
| Kitchen | 6 | 5 | IDR 5M |
| Cleaning/GRO | 1 | 1 | — |
| **Total** | **22** | **16** | **IDR ~13.5M/month** |

**Total monthly staff cost target:** ~IDR 107M (from current IDR 161M)

**How to get there:**
- Reduce waitstaff from 5 → 4 (one leaves naturally or moves to part-time)
- Reduce bar from 3 → 2 (consolidate with FOH during slow shifts)
- Kitchen from 6 → 5 (combine prep/cook roles)
- Use 2–3 part-time/casual staff for Friday/Saturday peak periods (pay per shift, no fixed cost)

**Risk warning:** Do not cut below 16 core. Service quality will suffer and repeat business will decline. The goal is efficiency, not starvation.

#### Step 1.2 — Lock In Lower Rent

April's rent was renegotiated to IDR 30M (from IDR 60M in Jan–Mar).

**Recommended action:** Approach the landlord with a proposal to lock in **IDR 30M/month through December 2027** in exchange for a longer lease commitment or a revenue-share clause.

At IDR 30M/month on IDR 500M revenue, rent = 6%, which is healthy for this market.
The 2026–2027 projection shows rent climbing back to IDR 40M+ — this will undo progress.

| Rent Scenario | Annual Cost | Difference vs IDR 30M |
|--------------|------------|----------------------|
| IDR 30M fixed (target) | IDR 360M | — |
| IDR 30M escalating to IDR 43M (current plan) | IDR 440M | **+IDR 80M/year** |

> **Bottom line**: Every IDR 1M reduction in monthly rent = IDR 12M/year directly to EBITDA.

#### Step 2.3 — Upselling & Average Check Growth (Staff Training)

**The single highest-ROI action in this entire plan.** Most guests currently spend IDR 200–230K for a main + drink. With simple techniques, this can reach IDR 250–260K.

**Training Module: The 3-Step Upsell**

| Step | Script | Target Sale |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 1. "Would you like to start with something?" | Nachos, Chips & Dip, or Chicken Fingers | +IDR 45–98K |
| 2. "Can I recommend a drink with that?" | Pairing suggestion (cocktail, beer, mocktail) | +IDR 35–85K |
| 3. "Save room for dessert?" | Churros, Brownie, Key Lime Pie | +IDR 49–59K |

**Target: 30% of guests order a starter + 40% order a dessert + 60% accept a drink pairing**

**Staff incentive:** Create a small bonus or competition for the highest average check per shift (IDR 50K bonus for the winning server each week) — this pays for itself many times over.

**Expected impact on average spend:**

| Current spend | After training | Lift | Revenue impact (2,000 guests/mo) |
|--------------|---------------|------|--------------------------------|
| IDR 210K | IDR 240K | +IDR 30K | **IDR 60M/month** |

#### Step 2.4 — Introduce Combo Bundles

Create 2–3 combo deals that simplify choice and increase average check:

| Combo | Items | Price | vs. Buying Separately | Margin Note |
|-------|-------|-------|----------------------|-------------|
| **"Lunch Deal"** (11am–4pm) | Any main + drink | IDR 20K off | Discount on drink | Drives off-peak traffic |
| **"Rosalita's Fiesta"** | Starter + Main + Drink | IDR 250K | 10–15% discount | Increases total spend |
| **"Date Night"** | 2 Mains + 1 Dessert + 2 Drinks | IDR 450K (or 500K for steak option) | 10–15% discount | Higher total ticket |

> **Important:** Combos should NOT discount high-margin items. The discount comes from the drink/dessert (high margin) subsidizing the main (lower margin). Total profit per guest should increase, not decrease.

#### Step 2.5 — Launch Daily Happy Hour (4–7PM)

**This is the single biggest untapped time slot.** Currently Rosalita's generates little revenue between lunch and dinner. The Thursday 5-8PM promo (shots + DJ) already proves there's demand for early-evening drinking.

**Happy Hour Offer (daily, 4–7PM):**
- **20% off all signature cocktails and margaritas** (Rosaritas, classic margs)
- **Half-price selected appetizers** (Chips & Dip, Nachos Queso, Chicken Fingers)
- **Beer special:** Bintang or Singaraja for IDR 35K (normally 60K/45K)

**Why this works:**
- Drinks have 75-85% margin — even at 20% off, they still have 55-65% margin
- Half-price appetizers drive food revenue at marginal cost
- Creates a "pre-dinner" occasion — guests often stay for dinner after happy hour
- Competes directly with Potato Head's and KU DE TA's late afternoon crowd

**Target metrics:**
- 15–25 happy hour covers/day (currently ~0)
- Average spend IDR 120–180K per HH guest
- 20% conversion of HH guests to staying for dinner

**Expected revenue: IDR 15–30M/month new revenue at 60%+ margin**

#### Step 2.6 — Launch Weekend Brunch (Sat–Sun, 10AM–2PM)

Seminyak has a **thriving brunch culture** and Rosalita's is missing it entirely. Every competitor from Taco Casa to Motel Mexicola offers brunch. The restaurant is currently closed before 11AM — this is zero-revenue time.

**Brunch Concept: "Rosalita's Weekend Fiesta Brunch"**
- **Hours:** Saturday–Sunday, 10AM–2PM
- **Format:** Build-Your-Own-Taco bar + Bottomless Mimosas/Margaritas
- **Price:** IDR 199K per person (food only) or IDR 299K with bottomless drinks
- **Menu:** 4 taco filling stations, Mexican rice & beans, churros station, fresh fruit

**Why this works:**
- Incremental revenue on currently-zero-revenue hours
- High-margin bottomless drinks drive profitability
- Differentiates from brunch competition (unique Mexican brunch angle)
- Uses existing kitchen capacity during off-peak morning hours

**Staffing:** 2-3 additional part-time staff (not core FTE) — paid per shift
**Marketing:** Instagram posts, hotel concierge flyers, Google Business Profile event

**Expected revenue: IDR 30–50M/month new revenue** (assuming 40-60 covers per brunch day at IDR 250K avg)

### M.4 StarPOINTS / StarXP / StarCHAIN — Deepening the Closed-Loop Ecosystem

#### M.4.1 Current State

Rosalita's already has StarPOINTS integration (confirmed by press release, Dec 2022, and live on website):

This is already a competitive advantage — no other Mexican restaurant in Seminyak offers blockchain-based loyalty.

#### M.4.2 The Full Ecosystem

| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **StarPOINTS** | Loyalty points earned at merchants (including Rosalita's) |
| **STARX** | Native token (ERC-777 → BSC BEP-20 migration) — tradeable, has DEX with ~10% tax |
| **StarXP** | Experience points from merchant spending — converted to STARX |
| **StarCHAIN** | Proprietary blockchain for ecosystem transactions |
| **StarWALLET** | Consumer app (wallet + merchant directory + token management) |
| **StarTRAVELLER** | Travel booking platform within the ecosystem |
| **DEX tax** | ~10% on STARX trades, with ~4% allocated to ecosystem development fund |

**The DEX tax is critical**: Every time STARX trades on the exchange, ~4% of the volume flows to the ecosystem fund. This fund pays for merchant incentives, marketing, and development. As a merchant in the ecosystem, Rosalita's can potentially access this fund for:
- Co-marketing budgets
- Customer acquisition subsidies
- Technology integration grants

#### M.4.3 Opportunity: Become the Primary F&B Merchant

**The gap**: Today, StarPOINTS is passive at Rosalita's — guests earn points, but the restaurant doesn't actively drive the behavior it wants.

**Proposed actions:**

1. **Front-load the loyalty** — Give bonus StarPOINTS for:
   - First visit (500 bonus points, worth ~IDR 25K)
   - Monday/Tuesday visits (double points — fills slow days)
   - Cocktail orders (triple points — drives highest-margin item)
   - Refer-a-friend (1,000 bonus points)

2. **StarXP acceptance** — Offer StarXP as a partial payment method:
   - Guests can pay up to 30-50% of their bill in StarXP
   - StarWORKS reimburses Rosalita's at ~90 cents on the dollar
   - Processing cost (~10%) is cheaper than GoFood (20-30%) or credit cards (~2-3% + FX)
   - Creates a reason for StarWALLET users to choose Rosalita's

3. **Cross-ecosystem exposure** — Get listed in StarWALLET's merchant directory as a featured merchant. StarTRAVELLER users booking Bali trips see Rosalita's as a recommended dining experience. Prestix.vip users get Rosalita's as the default dinner option.

### O.2 The 5-Year Clock

Indonesian tax law allows losses to be carried forward for **5 years maximum**. No carryback. No group offsetting.

- The turnaround plan targets profitability in mid-2027 — this aligns well with the 5-year window
- If realistic EBITDA targets are hit (IDR 150-200M/month by mid-2027), the full loss pool can be utilized within 12-18 months of reaching profitability

### O.3 General Indonesian Tax Context

Standard rates every F&B business in Bali should know:

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Corporate Income Tax** | 22% standard rate. Payable on annual net profit. |
| **Loss carryforward** | 5 years max. No carryback. No group offsetting. |
| **VAT (PPN)** | 11%. Input VAT on purchases is creditable against output VAT. |
| **Withholding Tax (Article 23)** | 2% on management/consulting/technical service fees to residents; 15% on interest/royalties. |
| **Monthly filings** | PPN due end of following month. PPh 21/23 by the 20th/10th. |
| **Annual CIT return** | Due April 30 for calendar-year filers. |

### O.4 Management Fee — Documentation Note

Rosalita pays IDR 6.25M/month (IDR 75M/year) in Admin/Management Fees. Best practice for any significant expense:

- Keep the underlying agreement or invoice from the service provider
- Ensure the fee corresponds to actual services received
- If paid to an Indonesian resident: 2% withholding tax (Article 23) applies and an 11% PPN invoice should be issued
